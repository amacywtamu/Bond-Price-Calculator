How Bond Pricing Works

A bond's price is the present value of its future cash flows. These cash flows include the regular coupon payments and the repayment of face value at maturity. When the market interest rate is higher than the coupon rate, the bond price is usually below face value. When the market interest rate is lower than the coupon rate, the bond price is usually above face value.

    Formula Reference

    Bond Price = PV of Coupon Payments + PV of Face Value

     

    PV of Coupon Payments = Coupon Payment × [1 − (1 + r)−n] / r

    PV of Face Value = Face Value / (1 + r)n

     

    Where:

      r = market interest rate per period

      n = total number of periods

     

    Special case (r = 0%):

      Bond Price = (Coupon Payment × n) + Face Value
